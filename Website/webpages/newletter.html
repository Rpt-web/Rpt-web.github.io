THEME PARK INSIDER

Do you like theme parks? Do you like newspapers? (probably not on the second one but still) Then do I have the thing for you. This week's article will be about the many changes coming to Disney World this year and next year and there are a LOT. First of all, the new villains show has come out and it looks AMAZING. The show has three main villains who all make claims for why they are the most misunderstood of all. (Capt. Hook, Maleficent, and Cruella De Vil) Each show has a different ending for each one being crowned as the most misunderstood. A new area for the villains is coming soon but we don’t know when. Movie Mayhem - The Downfall of Movie Theaters?

With streaming services on the rise, we have to begin to ask ourselves, “are movie theaters really worth it?” That is the question I intend to answer today so grab your popcorn because this is gonna be good. We start with the fact that movie theaters only show new movies while you can watch almost anything on a streaming service. While streaming services don’t usually get new movies until 2-3 months after they come out and have already started to leave theaters, it isn’t that bad of a wait. When you go to a movie theater, you go for the new movie, not for the one that they reshow a million times like Harry Potter. Another factor to take into account is the pricing, a normal netflix subscription costs around $10 dollars a month give or take, while a movie ticket costs $10-20 dollars. The ticket isn’t the only thing that is expensive though. You probably saw this coming from a mile away but the food/drink pricing is INSANE. The popcorn costs like $10 and the drinks are around the same price, like, hello? I could walk up to my local Walgreens and get a 6 pack of popcorn and a liter of coke for like $8. Does this mean that movie theaters are dying? I don’t think so because just being inside a movie theater is way cooler than sitting on your couch. Like, let's take a recent movie like (I can’t believe I'm talking about this movie) K-Pop Demon Hunters. This was a Netflix exclusive but eventually made its way to theaters. It made about 18 million dollars on the opening weekend. Now that might now seem like a lot compared to other movies, but remember, this movie was originally a Netflix exclusive. So 18 million dollars is very good. So, we’re back where we started, are movie theaters officially dead? No, definitely not. That's all I have for this edition.
